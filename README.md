# vickydurel.me

The code behind my personal site, [vickydurel.me](https://vickydurel.me) — and, honestly,
the first thing I've ever built purely to be seen.

## 🌒 Why it's here

For thirteen years, I built in the dark.

Not literally. I built for other people — their businesses, their deadlines, their names on
the result — and I kept myself out of the frame. The work was good. That was supposed to be
enough, and for a long while, it was.

I'm changing that now, on purpose. Not for attention — for something that outlasts the next
contract. I want the way I think to be findable, to stand on its own, apart from any single
client or project. If someone looks me up before we talk, let them meet me directly instead
of assembling me from fragments.

There's a quieter reason, too. I know how many good people stay hidden — not from laziness,
but from a small, stubborn fear of being seen. I lived inside that fear for years. If
stepping out from behind the work makes it less frightening for someone like me to do the
same, that alone is worth the exposure.

## 🪦 What the years taught me

The hard part was never the code. It was finishing.

I've started more than I've shipped. I've watched brilliant developers do the same — deliver
flawlessly for everyone else while quietly burying their own projects in a drawer. It's
rarely a skill problem. Things stall for softer reasons: the next idea always glitters
brighter than the one in your hands; a missing detail becomes a reason to go silent; the work
with no deadline and no client is the first to be abandoned. You end up with a graveyard of
things you genuinely loved. If you build, you probably have one too.

What changes it isn't more talent, and it certainly isn't more tools. It's staying. It's
choosing the dull middle of one thing over the bright beginning of the next. This site is me
practicing that in the open — something small, something mine, carried all the way to done.

## 🤖 Where I think AI fits

I use it every day. I am not afraid of it.

AI didn't come to replace us. It does what we taught it — an echo of every developer who ever
solved something hard and left the answer behind for the rest of us. It can carry more of
your weight than you'd believe. What it cannot do is care. It can't hold your judgment, your
taste, or the quiet responsibility of getting a thing right.

So build anyway. Keep learning. Keep adapting, because the ground never stops moving under
our feet. Lean on it as leverage, never as a replacement for becoming better at the craft.
Let it make you faster — never smaller. Used well, it doesn't erase your mark. It lets you
leave a deeper one.

## 👋 Who I am

Since 2013 I've helped businesses turn technology into real operating advantage — software
first, then digitized workflows, now the AI orchestration I focus on today — across Cameroon,
the UAE, and the UK. The tools change constantly. The standard never does: technology only
matters when it makes the work genuinely better.

That's who I am. This small site is where I stop hiding it.
